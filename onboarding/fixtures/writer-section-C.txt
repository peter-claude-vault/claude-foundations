Knowledge system. Yeah, fresh. I'm starting this vault from scratch — I had a folder of loose drafts on disk before, nothing organized, and I'm rebuilding from zero. Fresh vault, that's the call.

Organization is going to be topic-based. Not project-based, not engagement-based, topic-based. The reason is — every essay I write either revisits an old topic or starts a new one, and I keep wishing my notes were grouped by the idea, not by the publication date or which newsletter it ended up in. So I want a Topics top-level folder, and each topic folder holds essays in progress, outlines, drafts, and any research interview notes connected to that topic.

So topics are the structural unit. I'm not going to call them projects in the manuscript sense — even the manuscript will live as a set of topic folders, one per chapter's central concern, with chapter drafts organized as outline → draft → revision history. Has-structured-projects is probably false — what I'm building isn't a project tracker, it's a topic tracker.

Sensitive content. Honestly no, I don't have a sensitive-content concern. The content I publish is the content I write, and the in-progress drafts aren't secret. Skip the sensitive-isolation opt-in.

File types I want managed. Essays — every published or to-be-published piece. Drafts — the in-progress versions, separately, because a draft in revision is different from an essay that's gone out. Outlines — these are first-class for me, I outline before I draft and I keep them around to reuse. Interview notes — when the podcast starts those will need their own file type, separate from essays. And people pages for the small group I work with regularly. So roughly: essay, draft, outline, interview-note, people.

Folder structure. Topic at the top. Within a topic folder, sub-folders for outlines, drafts, and a publishing-log file that tracks which essays from that topic have actually shipped. The manuscript chapters get their own topic folders too.

Wikilink convention is title-as-link, normal Obsidian default.

One more thing. I do want a Transcripts folder for the podcast interview transcripts when those start coming in. Whisper-local is what I use for transcription and the raw transcripts will be heavy text files, I want them grouped centrally and then linked into the relevant topic folder per guest.
