Industry, honestly, just call it independent publishing. Or essayist, if that's a clean enough label. I've been doing this five years full-time, before that I was an editor at a literary magazine, so when I describe my seniority — not entry-level, I'm running my own thing now. Lead is fine. Working hours are mornings, eight to two Central, that's my draft window.

What am I working on. Three things, all parallel. The newsletter is the always-on project — Volume 3 is the current arc, weekly cadence, the audience is around four thousand subscribers right now and I'm watching that number every week. Each newsletter post is an essay; I keep an outline three or four weeks ahead, draft on Mondays and Tuesdays, edit Wednesday, publish Thursday morning.

Second is a manuscript — the working title is Quiet Hours, it's a long-form thing, my editor and I are deep into chapters four through six, that's where the draft revisions are heaviest right now. Editor sends marginal notes, I revise, send back, editor responds. The chapter draft history is its own folder in the vault because the revision tree gets thick.

Third is a podcast guest interview series — Casper, my producer, is booking the next four guests, we have outlines for each but the actual recording starts in two months, so for now it's all outline-stage prep, no recorded transcripts to manage yet.
