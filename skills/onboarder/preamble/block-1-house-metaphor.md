# Block 1 — House metaphor

> Think of this as moving into a new house. We are going to set up the foundation, the plumbing, the wiring, the rooms, and a smart-home system that runs on top of it. By the end, you will have an environment that not only houses your work, but autonomously runs work for you when you are not watching.

## Purpose

Orient via concrete intuition before the technical model lands. The house metaphor pre-loads two things the canonical diagram in Block 2 will then formalize:

- **Layered construction.** Foundation, plumbing, wiring, rooms, smart home — each layer depends on the one beneath it. Maps onto the three-layer mental model: experience configures architecture; architecture enables orchestration.
- **The point is not the house.** The closing clause — *autonomously runs work for you when you are not watching* — frames orchestration as the actual product, not the configuration setup. Adopters who internalize this avoid the failure mode of treating onboarding as the deliverable.

## Tone

Concrete, declarative, no hedging. The metaphor is intentionally domestic and physical (foundation, plumbing, wiring) to make the abstract architecture feel grounded. The closing line shifts register — *autonomously runs work* — which signals that the real subject is bigger than the metaphor.

## Bridge to Block 2

The metaphor lands first; the diagram replaces it second. Block 2 opens with: *Here is what that house actually looks like.* No transitional explanation needed — the metaphor primed the ground, and the diagram is the technical truth that pays it off.
